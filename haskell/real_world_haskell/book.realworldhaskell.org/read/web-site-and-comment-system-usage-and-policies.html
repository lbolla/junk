Thank you for your interest in commenting on our book! We are excited to have made it possible for you, our readers, to submit comments at the level of detail of every single paragraph and code example. Please feel welcome to make liberal use of the commenting system; we want to produce the best book we can. (Yes, your use of the comment system is subject to our User Agreement; see the section called “User Agreement” for details.)

We require Javascript and cookies

Our comment system makes extensive use of Javascript, and it helps to have cookies enabled. If you don't have Javascript enabled in your browser, you won't even see a hint of the existence of the comment system. Unfortunately, we don't have the time to spare to write a Javascript-free alternative. Patches welcome!

Using the comment system

Every paragraph ends with an unobtrusive anchor. When you click on it, it reveals the comments submitted by other readers. Click on the anchor again to hide the comments.

At the end of each list of comments sits a small form where you can enter comments of your own. We ask for just one piece of information in addition to your comment, which is your name. You're also welcome to provide a URL for your home page, blog, or what have you.

Why do we want your name?

We'd love to credit people who give particularly good feedback, and to do that, we need to know who to thank. You don't have to give us your real name, but if you don't, we can't credit you, no matter how helpful you may be.

We can't spam you, nor can anyone else

Notice that we don't ask for your email address? Nobody's going to spam you as a result of commenting on the book (unless for some reason you explicitly enter your email address somewhere).

How we review comments

After we've read a comment, we may mark it as “reviewed”; when we do this, you'll see a note next to a comment's title. Due to the huge volume of comments, we may not have time to mark all of them as reviewed.

When we do mark a comment as reviewed, it indicates that we've read and acted upon the comment, but that we haven't published an update yet. When a comment no longer applies to a particular paragraph, we'll hide it from view.

Subscribing to feeds

Our comment system isn't just a dead dump for snippets of text; it's a conversation between us and you. You'll notice that you can subscribe to an Atom feed for each web page, letting you see the latest comments, and there's also a feed for the book as a whole. Each comment published through the feed comes with a link that lets you see that comment in context, and respond to it.
